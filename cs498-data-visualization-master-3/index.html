Milano Telecom Visualization

Call Detail Record Analysis

 

Created by Tianfei Chen for CS498 Data Visualization

The Dataset

This telecommunications dataset contains user activities, includes SMS, Internet and Calls, (Call Detail Record) generated by an Italian cellular network provider over the city of Milano in November 2014.

I'm interested in finding out how people use phone in different time range and in different areas. About the Visualization

About the Visualization

Hybrid Structure This narrative visualization follows an interactive slideshow hybrid structure to guide users through various selected scenarios. This is the best choice for this dataset as it is too big to be presented to users directly. Data for each slide has been pre-processed and aggregated separately offline.

Scenes Four scenes have been included in this visualization using consistent template and layout. Each slide can be navigated using arrows at left or right, or using the navigation dots on top. Or use keyboard left and right buttons.

Annotations Interesting trends are annotated using orange color font and box in chart 1, 2 and 3.

Parameters Chart 2 and 3 include filters to display data from selected type of data source. Chart 4 includes a checkbox to reveal / hide grid that covers on top of the map.

Triggers The animation of chart 3 and 4 will be triggered automatically when the scene / slide is displayed. Changing filter in chart 3 will also trigger the chart animation.

Chrome is recommended. Chart 4 may be very slow or unresponsive in other browsers.

Different Usage Trends in Hours of Week

The first scatter chart shows Call activities drops significantly at night especially during week days. However it is difficult to see the details in this view as logarithmic scale has been applied on Y Axis.

So let's move on to next scene.

Different Usage Trends in Hours of Day

Internet SMS Call

The second bar chart clearly shows that Internet activities out-weight SMS and Call activities, and Internet activities happens all the time throughout the day, even after mid-night.

On the other hand, SMS and Call activities mostly occur in day time. The two peak periods are between 8AM – 12PM and 2PM – 6PM. People make much less SMS and Calls at night and sleeping time. The usage of Calls dropped quickly after business hours.

Different Squares in Hours of Week

University Downtown Nightlife Forest

The third line chart indicates that activities dropped significantly in university area over weekends. Such pattern cannot be found in nightlife and downtown areas.

This chart uses only a few selected squares as sample. See next scene for all squares.

All Squares in a Day

Show Grid Time 

The fourth grid chart will render all activities in City of Milano in a day. The city is divided into 100000 (100 x 100) squares according to the dataset. It is clear to see majority of activities happens in city areas, whereas activities cease in other areas. This is what has been observed in all previous charts.
